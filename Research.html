Undergraduate Thesis

Title: Assessing the Antimicrobial Effects on Silk Fabrics Treated with CuSO4 & Aleovera Gel.

Supervisor: Mr. Zakaria Alam Jiku, Assistant Professor, Dept. of Wet Processing Engineering, TECN
  • The objective was to assess and compare the antimicrobial properties of silk fabric treated with CuSO4 & Aleovera Gel.
  • The study involved characterization of treated silk fabric, antibacterial efficacy testing against Escherichia coli and Staphylococcus aureus, as well as an exploration of the underlying mechanisms.
  • Scanning electron microscopy (SEM) and energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (EDS) were employed to analyze the surface morphology.
  • Zone of inhibition tests and bacterial colony counting were used to assess antibacterial activity.
  • The results revealed significant antibacterial properties in copper sulfate-treated silk fabric, with rapid bactericidal kinetics.
